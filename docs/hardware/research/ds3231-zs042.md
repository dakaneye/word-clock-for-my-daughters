# DS3231 RTC — ZS-042 Module

## Part

- **Chip:** Maxim DS3231SN temperature-compensated crystal oscillator RTC
- **Module:** "ZS-042" — the ubiquitous low-cost Chinese DS3231 breakout with AT24C32 EEPROM and CR2032 battery holder.
- **Provenance for this project:** one spare from the Chelsea clock restock, plus a new 2-pack (assumed to be the same ZS-042 variant unless verified otherwise).

## Verified facts

### Supply voltage
- **Operating voltage: 2.3 V to 5.5 V** — works with 3.3 V or 5 V microcontrollers.
- The DS3231 has internal LDO behavior that handles either. Safe on our 3.3 V ESP32 rail.

### ⚠️ Critical: Battery-charging circuit bomb

**The ZS-042 module includes a 200 Ω resistor + 1N4148 diode in series between Vcc and the coin-cell battery positive terminal.** This is a trickle-charge circuit **intended for rechargeable LIR2032 cells.**

Most users receive ZS-042 modules with a **non-rechargeable CR2032** already installed (or install one themselves). **Attempting to charge a CR2032 can cause the battery to leak, vent gas, or explode.**

### Mitigation (required before installing a battery)

Remove the 200 Ω resistor:
1. Locate the resistor on the PCB (near the battery holder, in series on the positive lead).
2. Either **desolder it with a pencil iron**, **cut it with side cutters**, or **cut the trace between it and the rest of the circuit** with an X-Acto knife.
3. Verify with a multimeter: battery + terminal should show very high resistance (>1 MΩ, effectively open) to the Vcc rail after the mod.

This is a one-time permanent modification. After removal, a CR2032 runs the RTC's backup oscillator for ~8 years.

### I²C pullup resistors
- The tutorial does **not explicitly confirm** the pullup resistors on the ZS-042 module.
- **Community consensus / common experience:** ZS-042 boards typically include 4.7 kΩ-10 kΩ pullups on SDA and SCL to Vcc.
- **Before adding external pullups** to the PCB (Phase 3 PCB layout), measure SDA-to-Vcc and SCL-to-Vcc with the module powered off. If pullups are present, stacking more on the PCB drives the I²C bus too hard.

### I²C address
- DS3231: `0x68`
- AT24C32 EEPROM (also on the module): `0x57`
