# WS2812B (NeoPixel) Addressable RGB LED

## Part

- **Chip/LED:** WS2812B integrated-controller RGB LED (5050 SMD package)
- **Strip:** SEZO WS2812B 5m / 300 LEDs / 60 LEDs-per-meter / IP30 non-waterproof (from Phase 2 wishlist)

## Verified facts

### Current draw
- **Maximum per LED at full white: 60 mA @ 5 V** (confirmed Adafruit).
- **Practical rule of thumb for budgeting: 20 mA per LED** for typical mixed-color animation — "it's rare for all pixels to be turned on that way."
- For 25 LEDs on this clock:
  - Absolute worst case: 25 × 60 mA = **1.5 A**
  - Realistic average at bedtime dim: 25 × 20 mA × 0.1 = **50 mA**
  - Default warm white at full brightness: roughly 25 × 40 mA = **1.0 A**

### Logic level — ⚠️ critical finding
- Adafruit explicitly warns: **"a 3.3 V signal may not work reliably."**
- The WS2812B data-in HIGH threshold is **70% of Vdd** — so **3.5 V minimum** when Vdd is 5 V.
- ESP32 outputs 3.3 V HIGH, which is 200 mV BELOW the threshold.
- **Sometimes this works** (depends on strip batch, ambient temperature, cable length) but it is not reliable.
- **Solutions:**
  1. **Preferred: 74HCT245 (or 74HCT125) level shifter** between MCU and strip. HCT family has TTL-compatible inputs — reliably reads 3.3 V as HIGH, outputs 5 V HIGH. Cost ~$1.
  2. Drop NeoPixel Vdd to ~3.7 V (e.g., via a single diode in series) — brings the HIGH threshold down to ~2.6 V. Lower brightness; not recommended for a keeper build.

### Capacitor on power rail
- **"A large capacitor (500-1000 µF at 6.3 V or higher) across the + and – terminals"** is required.
- **Connect capacitor before applying power.**
- Absorbs inrush when all LEDs power up white; protects individual pixels from voltage spikes.

### Power-up safety
- **Avoid bench power supplies** if possible — voltage spikes can destroy pixels.
- If using a bench supply: power on, let voltage stabilize, **then** connect pixels (GND first).
- USB-C @ 5 V is fine as a source, as is the chosen design.

### Power distribution for longer strips
- "Aim for 1 meter or less distance from any pixel to a power connection."
- For 25 LEDs on a 7"×7" face (total strip run ~1.5 m), a single power feed at one end is acceptable but a second feed at the far end improves color consistency.

### Series resistor on data line
- Not called out explicitly by Adafruit in this page, but community practice: **300 Ω in series on DIN** between MCU (or level shifter) and the first pixel. Protects the first WS2812B from voltage spikes on the data line. Already listed in pinout.md.
